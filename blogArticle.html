Types of Lighting

The type of lighting in your room can influence your productivity. Lighting can help you create your ideal atmosphere. To do that, you'll need to have a basic understanding of the type of lighting you use. Lighting can be generalized into three broad categories: task, ambient or general, and accent lighting.

1. Task Lighting

Task lighting directs light to a specific area to make completing close range activities easier. For example, you may use task lighting when you read, write, sew, or cook. Task lights provide enough lighting that lets you focus. Depending on the activity you are using the task light for, you may want to consider the intensity of the light.

2. Ambient Lighting

Ambient lighting is often referred to as general lighting as it is the primary source that provides light to a specific interior space. When choosing an ambient lighting, consider the lighting intensity, color temperature, and the placement of the light in the room.

3. Accent Lighting

Accent lighting is used to draw attention to a specific object or area. For example, this type of lighting is often used in museums, in gardens to highlight a specific field of flowers, and in rooms to highlight showcases like photographs and wine cases.
